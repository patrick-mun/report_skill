# Structure — research / funding report

Default section plan for a research report or a funding application. If the user supplies their own outline, **their outline wins** — follow it exactly.

1. **Title, authors, affiliation, date, version**
2. **Executive summary / abstract** — finding, problem, proposed solution, deliverables (3–5 points).
3. **Context and research question** — state of the art, blind spot, research question.
4. **Objectives** — primary objective + secondary objectives (clinical, scientific, territorial).
5. **Project design overview** — overall architecture, cohorts, modules.
6. **Methodology** — recruitment, sampling, measurements, analyses, parameters, thresholds.
7. **Algorithms / models** (if relevant) — principle, formulation, validation.
8. **Resources produced** — data, panels, reference materials delivered.
9. **Operational organization** — partners, governance, data flow, compliance (GDPR/HDS), security.
10. **Budget and resources** — line items, scenarios, infrastructure, personnel.
11. **Expected impacts** — clinical, diagnostic, scientific, territorial.
12. **Risks and limitations** — identified risks + mitigation measures.
13. **Conclusion** — summary of need, contribution, conditions for success, perspectives.
14. **Annexes** — detailed bibliography, statistical methodology, detailed budget, governance/GDPR/HDS.

Each numbered section may carry sub-sections; keep the heading hierarchy to three levels (see `style-guide.md`).
