# Structure — professional / business report

Default section plan for a professional report, briefing, or memo. If the user supplies their own outline, **their outline wins** — follow it exactly.

1. **Title page** — title, subject, author(s)/organization, date, version.
2. **Executive summary** — subject, findings, main recommendation (3–5 points).
3. **Context and objectives** — why this report, scope.
4. **Analysis** — findings, data, interpretation (thematic sub-sections).
5. **Recommendations** — prioritized list of actions.
6. **Action plan** — who does what, deadlines (table).
7. **Conclusion** — summary and next steps.
8. **Annexes** — detailed data, sources, methodology.

Keep the heading hierarchy to three levels (see `style-guide.md`). Use callouts for the one or two takeaways that matter most per section.
