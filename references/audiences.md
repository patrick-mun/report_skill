# Audiences — adapting one report to different readers

The same project must speak to different readers. The substance stays constant; the depth, vocabulary, emphasis, and what is foregrounded change. Use this guide when producing audience-specific versions, or when ordering the layers of a single layered document.

## General rule

Keep the facts identical across versions. Only change: level of technical detail, vocabulary, which sections lead, and which are summarized vs. detailed vs. moved to annex.

## Profiles

### Funders / decision-makers
- **Goal:** decide whether to fund. They read the summary, maybe the budget.
- **Lead with:** problem, impact, feasibility, budget, risks.
- **Depth:** high-level. Methodology compressed to one paragraph + annex link.
- **Vocabulary:** plain, outcome-oriented. Expand all acronyms.
- **Keep visible:** budget scenarios, timeline, expected impact, risk mitigation.

### Scientists / experts
- **Goal:** assess scientific validity and rigor.
- **Lead with:** the question, the design, the method.
- **Depth:** full. Preserve parameters, formulas, thresholds, validation design, and exact notation from the methodology sources. Do not simplify the algorithm.
- **Vocabulary:** domain-precise; technical terms expected. Keep a glossary for cross-disciplinary terms only.
- **Keep visible:** methodology, selection algorithm, statistical validation, data resources produced, bibliography. Budget can be summarized.

### Clinicians
- **Goal:** understand diagnostic / therapeutic relevance.
- **Lead with:** clinical and pharmacogenetic consequences, patient pathway.
- **Depth:** medium on method, high on clinical implications.
- **Vocabulary:** clinical; lighter on bioinformatics internals.

### Institutions / general public
- **Goal:** grasp the stakes and territorial value.
- **Lead with:** the territorial issue, partners, governance, benefits for the population.
- **Depth:** vulgarized. Replace jargon, use analogies, expand every acronym.
- **Keep visible:** motivation, partners, governance, regional impact, ethics (consent, GDPR/HDS).

## Layered single document (ordering)

When producing one navigable document instead of separate versions, order the layers so each reader can stop when satisfied:

1. Executive summary (funders / everyone).
2. Context & objectives (everyone).
3. Scientific approach & method (scientists) — the bulk.
4. Validation (scientists).
5. Resources produced & impact (clinicians + scientists).
6. Budget & timeline (funders).
7. Governance, ethics, risks (institutions).
8. Annexes (full technical detail, bibliography).
